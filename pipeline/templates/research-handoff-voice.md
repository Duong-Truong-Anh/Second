# Research Brief NN: Voice & Register — <Mentor Name>

> You are a research agent. This brief is self-contained: everything you need is here.
> Do not assume access to any other files, briefs, or prior conversation.
>
> **This brief is about HOW the mentor talks, not WHAT they teach.** You are capturing
> register, cadence, characteristic phrasings, metaphors, and explanatory structure —
> the texture of their expression — so a future agent can teach *in their voice*.
> The frameworks themselves are other briefs' job; ignore them except as examples of
> *how* the mentor phrases things.

## Mission
Document the way **<Mentor Name>** communicates: their register and stance, how they
build an explanation, their recurring metaphors and exact phrasings, their vocabulary
and rhythm, and where they're blunt vs gentle.

## Who the mentor is
<2–4 sentences: identity, domain, where they publish. Enough to disambiguate.>

## Why this is well-sourced (unlike teaching behavior)
Voice lives in **monologue content** — videos, short-form clips, posts, newsletters,
talks, podcast answers. This exists in volume. Mine it directly. (Their 1-on-1 mentoring
*behavior* is a different, often unsourced thing — not your job here.)

## Source guidance
<Their highest-volume monologue channels: YouTube, TikTok/IG/Shorts, newsletter, blog,
LinkedIn, podcast guest spots. Point at specific high-density examples from the scan.>

## What to extract
1. **Register & stance** — blunt/warm/contrarian/wry; peer vs coach vs provocateur;
   emotional temperature. Quote lines that exemplify it.
2. **Explanatory structure** — their default move (analogy-first? contrarian hook →
   reframe? numbers-first? story → lesson?). Show the shape with ≥2 real examples.
3. **Signature metaphors & phrases** — their actual recurring images and one-liners,
   verbatim, with sources. Note catchphrases they repeat across content.
4. **Structural tics** — recurring formats ("❌ vs ✅", "X person vs Y person",
   listicles, "here's the thing though…").
5. **Vocabulary & cadence** — words they reach for; sentence length/rhythm; jargon they
   *avoid*; registers they refuse.
6. **Blunt vs soft** — where they sharpen, where they encourage.

## Rules (non-negotiable)
1. **Quote, don't paraphrase.** Voice is in the exact words — prefer `[stated-verbatim]`
   with the source. Paraphrase loses the thing you're capturing.
2. **Cite everything** (URL, video + timestamp, post date).
3. **Voice, not biography.** Capture how they sound, not claims about their life — and
   never invent either.
4. **Report thinness honestly.** If a mentor's content is sparse or generic, say so —
   a thin voice profile is a real finding (it tells sessions to stay light-touch).
