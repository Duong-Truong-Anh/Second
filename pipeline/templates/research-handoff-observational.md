## Behavior extraction protocol
1. **Per artifact, record concrete instances** in the form
   *situation → the mentor's move → outcome*. Example shape: "learner had underpriced
   a project and was afraid to correct it → mentor asked X, then had them do Y on the
   call → learner committed to Z." Capture exact questions and phrasings verbatim
   where possible, with timestamps.
2. **Generalize only afterward.** A claimed pattern ("they always start by asking
   about money") requires ≥2 independent instances, all cited. Single instances are
   reported as instances, not patterns.
3. **Every claim carries its tier** (T1/T2/T3). Where a T2 self-description is
   corroborated or *contradicted* by T1 observation, report that explicitly —
   contradictions are among the most valuable findings.
4. **Currency check.** Note artifact dates; if their style visibly differs between
   older and newer material, report the shift rather than averaging it away.
5. **Generic pedagogy is forbidden.** "Good mentors listen actively" is noise unless
   you observed *this mentor* doing a specific, citable version of it.
6. **Report gaps as gaps.** Dimensions of mentoring behavior the artifacts never show
   (e.g., no artifact shows them handling repeated failure) are reported as not
   observed — never filled in with plausible invention.

## Dimensions to cover (where the material allows)
1. Opening moves with someone new — what they ask/assess first
2. Diagnosing: how they locate the real problem behind the stated one
3. Responding to stuck, confused, or discouraged learners
4. Feedback delivery: direct vs cushioned; demonstrate vs critique vs assign
5. Adjusting when advice isn't landing
6. Sequencing: what they insist on first, what they defer
7. What triggers pushback, and the form the pushback takes
8. Homework: what they assign and how they follow up
