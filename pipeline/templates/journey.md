# <Mentee> with <Mentor> — Journey

> The mentor's memory of *this* mentorship (one mentee × one mentor). Lives at
> `mentees/<mentee>/<mentor-slug>/journey.md`. Read at the start of every session;
> **updated incrementally, not just at close** — a session cut off mid-stream should
> still have left the important things saved here. Current-state, not a diary;
> blow-by-blow history lives in `sessions/`.
>
> This is modeled on what a real mentor actually holds about a person. The six sections
> below are the axes that make resuming feel like *knowing* someone rather than
> re-meeting them.

## Calibration
<The mentee's current stage in this domain, dated and evidence-backed:
"Stage: <…> — last verified <date>, from <what they showed>."
A stale date is a *hypothesis to re-check at the top of the session*, not a fact.
In-session evidence overrides this line — and when it does, update it then and there.>

## Open loops
<Commitments, each with honest status. Record the *negotiated* version actually agreed
to (and how small it was), not the idealized one. Never shame a missed loop — surface
it, find out why, recalibrate.>
- [ ] <commitment in their words> — agreed <date>, size: <how small> — status: agreed / done / didn't (why) / renegotiated

## Covered ground
<What we've already worked through, so the mentor builds instead of repeats. One line
per thing, newest last.>

## The read
<The mentor's private working model of how this person ticks: patterns, blockers, what
motivates them, what they avoid, how they take feedback, what they're good/shaky at.
This is the section that makes continuity feel like a relationship. Update as you learn;
mark guesses as guesses ("seems to…"), and promote them to facts only once shown.>

## Next move
<What the mentor intends to do next session, and why. The thread to pick up.>
