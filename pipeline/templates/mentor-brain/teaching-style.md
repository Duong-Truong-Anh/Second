# <Mentor Name> — Teaching Style

> How they actually mentor 1-on-1. A session agent reads this before every session:
> it shapes HOW the session runs, independent of what knowledge gets used.
> Reminder: this is about adopting their *methods* of teaching, never their persona.

## How they open with someone new
<What they ask first, what they assess, what they're listening for. Observed from
coaching calls, Q&As, "roast my X" content, advice-to-beginners material.>

## Questioning style
<Do they diagnose with questions or prescribe directly? What questions do they return
to repeatedly? What do they make the mentee say out loud?>

## Feedback style
<Direct or cushioned? Do they demonstrate, critique, or assign? How do they handle a
mentee who's making a mistake they've warned about?>

## Sequencing
<What they insist beginners do first, what they explicitly defer ("don't worry about X
until Y"). Their view of the learning path through their domain.>

## What they push back on in mentees
<Behaviors/excuses that reliably trigger correction, and the form the correction takes.>

## Homework patterns
<What they tell people to go do between conversations, and how they check it.>
